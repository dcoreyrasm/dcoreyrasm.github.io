Sometimes typing is the slow part, not the thinking. All four of these tools let you just talk instead, and the voice experience across the board just got a lot more natural, less like a walkie-talkie, more like an actual conversation. A spoken answer can still be confidently wrong, the same as a typed one, so treat anything important the same way and double-check it before you act on it.

## Claude: Voice mode

![Claude: Voice mode](/assets/blog/2026-07-22/claude.png){: .section-image }

Claude has a free voice mode built right into the app, so you can talk through an idea out loud instead of typing it.

1. Open Claude on the web or in the mobile app and start a chat.
2. On the web, tap the sound wave icon in the lower right of the chat window. On mobile, tap the voice mode icon next to the microphone in the text box.
3. On mobile, pick a voice you like.
4. Start talking. Claude answers out loud once it hears you pause, and you can keep going back and forth.

**When to reach for it:** reach for this when you want to think out loud, like talking through a decision on a walk, without stopping to type.

Source: [Claude Help Center](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/11101966-use-voice-mode)

## ChatGPT: GPT-Live

![ChatGPT: GPT-Live](/assets/blog/2026-07-22/chatgpt.png){: .section-image }

ChatGPT just rolled out a new voice model called GPT-Live that listens and talks at the same time, so it feels much less like a walkie-talkie and more like a real conversation. It is still rolling out as of July 2026, so what you see may vary a bit by device.

1. Open ChatGPT in the app or at chatgpt.com.
2. Tap the Voice button in the message box.
3. Start talking. GPT-Live can respond mid-sentence, sit quietly while you think, and pull in a smarter model in the background for a harder question without breaking the flow.
4. Tap the stop or X icon to end the conversation and return to text.

**When to reach for it:** reach for this for a real back-and-forth, like talking out a schedule conflict or practicing what you will say in a hard conversation, since it handles interruptions and pauses more naturally than the older voice mode did.

Source: [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-live/)

## Copilot: Copilot Voice

![Copilot: Copilot Voice](/assets/blog/2026-07-22/copilot.png){: .section-image }

The free, personal Copilot has its own voice feature, so you can talk to it the way you would call a friend for a quick answer.

1. Open the Copilot app, or go to copilot.com, and find the microphone icon in the composer.
2. Tap the microphone icon to start voice mode.
3. Allow microphone access if it is your first time.
4. Wait for Copilot's greeting, then start talking. Tap the microphone again to mute or unmute, and tap the X to end the conversation.

**When to reach for it:** good for a quick spoken question when your hands are busy, like checking a fact while you cook (or hands-free while driving, where that is legal).

Source: [Microsoft Support](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-copilot/using-copilot-voice-with-microsoft-copilot)

## Gemini: Gemini Live

![Gemini: Gemini Live](/assets/blog/2026-07-22/gemini.png){: .section-image }

Gemini Live is a free, natural voice conversation mode built right into the Gemini app.

1. Open the Gemini app on your phone.
2. At the bottom, tap the Live button, or swipe left.
3. Follow any on-screen prompts the first time.
4. Start talking. You can also just say "Hey Google, let's talk Live" without opening the app first.

**When to reach for it:** reach for this for a longer, free-flowing conversation, like talking through travel plans out loud while you pack.

Source: [Gemini Apps Help](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/15274899)

## One thing to keep in mind

A spoken answer can sound confident and still be wrong, the same as a typed one. For anything that matters, like a fact, a number, or advice you plan to act on, check it before you rely on it.
