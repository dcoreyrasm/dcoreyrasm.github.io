You already keep your life in Gmail, a calendar, Google Drive, or OneDrive. All four tools can now reach into those apps directly instead of you copying and pasting everything back in by hand. One thing worth knowing before you turn a connection on: it can see real, personal information, so only connect an account you would not mind an assistant looking into.

## Claude: Connectors

![Claude: Connectors](/assets/blog/2026-07-19/claude.png){: .section-image }

Claude can link to Gmail, Google Calendar, and Google Drive, so it can look things up in your own accounts instead of you pasting them in.

1. In Claude.ai or the Claude app, click the plus sign in the chat box.
2. Hover over Connectors, then click the plus button to open the directory (or choose Manage connectors).
3. Pick Gmail, Google Calendar, or Google Drive, select Connect, then sign in and choose what to share.
4. Back in the chat, toggle the connector on and just ask, like "check my calendar for anything Thursday afternoon" or "find the RSVP list in my Drive."

**When to reach for it:** when the answer already lives in your own email, calendar, or files, and you would rather ask than dig it up yourself.

Source: [Claude Help Center](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/11176164-use-connectors-to-extend-claude-s-capabilities)

## ChatGPT: Apps in ChatGPT

![ChatGPT: Apps in ChatGPT](/assets/blog/2026-07-19/chatgpt.png){: .section-image }

ChatGPT can connect to apps like Google Drive, so you can pull in a file from your own account instead of uploading it every time.

1. Open ChatGPT and click the plus button in the message box.
2. Open the app directory, find Google Drive (or another app you use), and select Connect.
3. Sign in and choose what ChatGPT is allowed to see.
4. Back in the chat, reference the app and ask your question, like "find last month's lease in my Drive and tell me the renewal date."

**When to reach for it:** good when what you need is already sitting in one of your everyday apps, so you skip the upload and go straight to the question.

Source: [OpenAI Help Center](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/9309188-add-files-from-connected-apps-in-chatgpt)

## Copilot: connecting Copilot to your other accounts

![Copilot: connecting Copilot to your other accounts](/assets/blog/2026-07-19/copilot.png){: .section-image }

The free, personal version of Copilot can connect to OneDrive, Outlook.com, Google Drive, and Gmail, so it can check your own email or files when you ask.

1. Go to copilot.microsoft.com, or open the Copilot app, and sign in with your personal Microsoft account.
2. Open your profile and select Connectors.
3. Pick the service you want, like Gmail or Google Drive, and select Connect.
4. Sign in to that account, choose what to share, then ask something like "check my Outlook calendar for Thursday afternoon."

**When to reach for it:** this is the free personal Copilot, not the work version, so it is the one to use for your own inbox and files, not a company account.

Source: [Microsoft Support](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/connecting-microsoft-copilot-to-other-services-cc06f6ef-a885-4187-9380-712bb4cabac8)

## Gemini: Connected Apps

![Gemini: Connected Apps](/assets/blog/2026-07-19/gemini.png){: .section-image }

Gemini can reach into apps like Gmail, Calendar, and Drive with an @ mention, so one answer can pull from more than one place.

1. Go to gemini.google.com and sign in.
2. Open Settings & help, then Connected apps.
3. Find the app you want, like Gmail or Google Calendar, and turn it on.
4. In a chat, type @ followed by the app's name, then ask your question, like "@Gmail find the email with my flight confirmation."

**When to reach for it:** reach for this when your answer needs more than one source at once, like your calendar and your inbox together.

Source: [Gemini Apps Help](https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/13695044)

## One thing to keep in mind

Connecting an account is not a small thing to turn on. Only connect an inbox, calendar, or drive you would be comfortable with an assistant actually reading, and check what it is allowed to see before you ask it anything sensitive.
