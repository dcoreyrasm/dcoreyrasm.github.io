That family budget, that trip-expense tracker, that sign-up sheet. They all sit somewhere gathering dust, because getting one answer out means digging through formulas you do not remember writing. All four tools can now sit inside or next to a spreadsheet and just answer a plain question about it. Here is one way to do that in each, plus an honest note on what it takes to get access.

## Claude: ask Claude for Excel about your workbook

![Claude: ask Claude for Excel about your workbook](/assets/blog/2026-07-13/claude.png){: .section-image }

Claude for Excel is an add-in that sits inside Excel and answers questions about the workbook you already have open, pointing to the exact cells it used.

1. In Excel, find the Microsoft Marketplace listing for "Claude for Microsoft 365" and click Get it now to install the add-in.
2. Open your spreadsheet on web, Windows, Mac, or iPad, and open the Claude sidebar from the ribbon.
3. Sign in with your Claude account, then ask a plain question about the sheet, for example "which month did we go over budget, and by how much."
4. Check the cells Claude points to before you trust the answer.

**When to reach for it:** when you already keep a working spreadsheet and want an answer without hunting through your own formulas. As of mid-2026 this needs a Claude Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise plan, not the free tier.

Source: [Claude Help Center](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/12650343-use-claude-for-excel)

## ChatGPT: upload a spreadsheet and ask ChatGPT to dig in

![ChatGPT: upload a spreadsheet and ask ChatGPT to dig in](/assets/blog/2026-07-13/chatgpt.png){: .section-image }

ChatGPT can take a spreadsheet or CSV you upload and run real analysis on it, totals, trends, and charts, not just a summary.

1. Open ChatGPT and start a new chat.
2. Click the attachment icon and upload your spreadsheet or CSV export, a bank statement, a trip budget, whatever you are tracking.
3. Ask for what you want, for example "chart our spending by category" or "what did we average on groceries each week."
4. Follow up to go deeper, like "now show it without the one-time purchases."

**When to reach for it:** when your numbers live in a messy export rather than a workbook you maintain, and you want it turned into something readable. As of mid-2026, file uploads and data analysis need a ChatGPT Plus plan or higher.

Source: [OpenAI Help Center](https://help.openai.com/en/articles/8437071-data-analysis-with-chatgpt)

## Copilot: chat with Copilot inside Excel

![Copilot: chat with Copilot inside Excel](/assets/blog/2026-07-13/copilot.png){: .section-image }

Copilot in Excel sits in the same window as your spreadsheet and can answer questions, build a chart, or clean up the data without you writing a formula. This is Microsoft 365 Copilot, not the free consumer version.

1. Open your workbook in Excel and select the Copilot icon in the lower right corner.
2. Choose Chat mode for answers without changes, or Edit mode to let Copilot update the sheet directly.
3. Ask a real question, for example "which category are we overspending in this month" or "build a pivot table of spending by person."
4. Review the plan Copilot proposes before it runs, and watch it work in real time.

**When to reach for it:** when the sheet already lives in Excel and you want Copilot to build something, a chart, a pivot table, a formula, not just talk about it. Requires a Microsoft 365 subscription that includes Copilot.

Source: [Microsoft Support](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/excel/copilot/get-started-with-copilot-in-excel)

## Gemini: have Gemini build the spreadsheet for you

![Gemini: have Gemini build the spreadsheet for you](/assets/blog/2026-07-13/gemini.png){: .section-image }

Instead of opening a blank sheet, you can describe what you need and have Gemini in Sheets build it, formulas and all.

1. Open Google Sheets. The Build side panel opens automatically on the right, or open it from the toolbar.
2. Type what you want, for example "build a trip expense tracker for four people with columns for who paid, category, and amount owed."
3. Answer any follow-up questions Gemini asks, then review the plan and template outline.
4. Let it generate the sheet, and ask a follow-up like "add a tab that totals what each person owes" to keep building on it.

**When to reach for it:** when you are starting from nothing and would rather describe the tracker than build it cell by cell. As of mid-2026 this needs Gemini Alpha, Google AI Pro or Ultra, or the Workspace Experiments program, not the Gemini access most people have by default.

Source: [Google Docs Editors Help](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/16959434?hl=en)

## One thing to keep in mind

An AI reading your spreadsheet can still misread a formula or miscount a range, especially on a sheet with a lot of manual patching over the years. Spot-check anything that touches real money, like a total or a balance, against the sheet itself before you act on it.
