"I took a blank slate as an invitation to build something that truly serves Yale College. With no preset boundaries, I forged new collaborations, led strategic projects, and integrated communities across the university."

I'm an IT leader who builds the digital connective tissue for higher education. My career spans 18+ years at Yale, where I've grown from Programmer Analyst to Senior Director, managing a $981K annual IT budget, overseeing 50+ websites, and leading strategic initiatives that connect technology to student success.

Before joining Yale, I began my teaching career as an adjunct instructor at Albertus Magnus College, then the University of Phoenix, and later Rasmussen University. I taught courses in SQL, database management, e-commerce, and business systems. That experience still shapes how I lead today. I explain things clearly, I coach people through complexity, and I focus on building solutions that people can actually use.

At Yale, I co-developed Designing Your Career, a platform now used by 7,100+ students across 83 institutions. That work earned the Linda K. Lorimer Presidential Award for Distinguished Service in 2024. I've also led efforts that resulted in $130K in annual vendor savings, modernized student housing operations generating up to $1.4M in cost savings, and implemented PCI-compliant point-of-sale systems across 20 departments.

I'm also a contributor to multiple editions of Processes, Systems, and Information: An Introduction to MIS, a textbook used in university classrooms nationwide.

The throughline in my work is simple. When technology is built with intention and grounded in real needs, it can make a meaningful difference in people's lives.
